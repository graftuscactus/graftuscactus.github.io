Care instructions for your grafted cacti

Plant in well-draining soil. Commercial cactus soil is okay, though I prefer to just use a half and half mixture of perlite and any other commercial soil mix such as miracle grow or pro-mix. Adding perlite to your soil will increase drainage and keep the roots from staying wet for too long.

Slowly acclimate cactus to natural sunlight if you plan to have it outdoors in full sun. Increase amount of natural full sunlight by about 1 hour per day (starting from 1 hour) to avoid sunburn when you first receive the cactus.

Be careful of the spines on the graft and on the grafting stock (they hurt!).

Do not worry of the Pereskiopsis drops some of its leaves during shipping, this will not affect the growth of your grafted cactus. If the pereskiopsis ever puts off a shoot to grow more pereskiopsis, you can remove it to cause the pereskiopsis to put all of its energy into the grafted cactus.

Allow the surface of the soil to dry out between watering. It can help to plant the cactus is a clay pot, but I use plastic and they work fine as long as your pot has drainage in the bottom and you allow soil to dry between waterings.
